\section*{Truth table algorithms}
\textbf{Enter :} f (logic function), variable (a,b,c,d,$\cdots$)\\
\textbf{Exit :} f, f1 (first canonic form), f2 (second canonic form)\\
First, we enter a binary values for each variable 0 (False) and 1 (True).\\
Then, we calculate it according  to these calculation rules :
\subsection*{Base operation}
\begin{itemize}
	\item[-] NOT ($\bar{a}$) : inverse or a complementary.
	\item[-] AND ($ab$ or $a \dot b$) : return 1 if a and b is 1, else return 0.
	\item[-] OR ($a + b$) : return 1 if one of a and b is 1, else if a and b are 0 he returns 0.
\end{itemize}
\subsection*{Base property}
\begin{itemize}
	\item[-] Involution : $\bar{\bar{a}}=a$
	\item[-] Idempotence : $\left\{\begin{array}{ccc}a + a & = & a \\ a \cdot a = a\end{array}\right.$
	\item[-] Neutral element : $\left\{\begin{array}{ccc}a & = & a \cdot 1 = 1 \cdot a \\ a & = & a + 0 = 0 + a\end{array}\right.$
	\item[-] Absorbent : $\left\{\begin{array}{ccc}a + 1  = 1 + a & = & 1 \\ a \cdot 0 = 0 \cdot a = 0\end{array}\right.$
	\item[-] Associativity : $\left\{\begin{array}{ccc}(a + b) + c & = & a + (b + c) \\ (a \cdot b) \cdot c & = & a \cdot (b \cdot c)\end{array}\right.$
	\item[-] Distributivity : $\left\{\begin{array}{ccc}a \cdot (b + c) & = & a \cdot + a \cdot c \\ a + (b \cdot c) & = & (a + b) \cdot (a +c)\end{array}\right.$
	\item[-] Morgan's rule : $\left\{\begin{array}{ccc} \overline{a + b} & = & \bar{a} \cdot \bar{b} \\ \overline{a \cdot b} & = & \bar{a} + \bar{b} \end{array}\right.$
	\item[-] Optimisation : $\left\{\begin{array}{ccc}a + \bar{a}b & = & a + b \\ a + bc & = & (a + b)(a + c)\end{array}\right.$
\end{itemize}

\section*{Canonic form}
\textbf{Minterms :} a group of variable may be linked by AND (ex : $abc$).\\
\textbf{Maxterms :} a gorup of variable may be linked by OR (ex : $a + b + c$).\\ \\
\textbf{$1^{st}$ canonic form :} union (OR) of minterms\\
\textbf{$2^{th}$ canonic form :} intersection (AND) of maxterms
